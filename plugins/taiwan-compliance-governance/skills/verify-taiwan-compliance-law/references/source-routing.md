# Taiwan Legal Source Routing

## Authority order

1. Current statute or regulation text from the Laws & Regulations Database of the Republic of China or the competent authority.
2. Official gazette, order, interpretation, inspection rule, or subordinate regulation from the competent authority.
3. Official Executive Yuan, Presidential Office, or ministry material confirming promulgation and effective dates.
4. Official guidance, FAQ, or handbook, clearly labelled as guidance rather than statutory text.

Do not treat law-firm articles, blogs, news reports, aggregators, or search snippets as legal authority. They may only identify a question that must be verified against an official source.

## Topic routing

| Topic | Primary authority or official source |
| --- | --- |
| Employment, work rules, discrimination, harassment, non-compete | Ministry of Labor and official law database |
| Occupational safety, contractors, construction, cranes and lifting | Occupational Safety and Health Administration, Ministry of Labor |
| Personal data and employee monitoring | Official law database and the competent central authority for the regulated activity |
| Trade secrets and intellectual property | Ministry of Economic Affairs and Taiwan Intellectual Property Office |
| AI governance | National Science and Technology Council, Ministry of Digital Affairs, and the competent sector authority |
| Company vehicles and road safety | Ministry of Transportation and Communications and official law database |
| Government procurement, integrity, and anti-corruption | Public Construction Commission, Ministry of Justice, and official law database |
| Environmental controls | Ministry of Environment and the competent local authority |

## Effective-status definitions

- `current`: the cited provision is in force for the relevant facts and date.
- `future-effective`: officially promulgated or amended, but the relevant provision starts later.
- `superseded`: no longer the controlling text for the relevant date.
- `pending-verification`: the official text, effective date, or applicability facts are incomplete.

## Applicability gates

Check only the gates relevant to the issue:

- employing entity, business category, and operating location;
- workforce size and composition;
- employee, contractor, subcontractor, visitor, or other protected population;
- construction project type, value, duration, or scale;
- prime contractor, contractor, subcontractor, or equipment-owner responsibility;
- machinery type, rated capacity, inspection status, operator qualification, and work environment;
- personal-data category, purpose, notice, consent or statutory basis, access, retention, and cross-border transfer;
- incident date, amendment date, effective date, and any transition period.
